# Spanish Reader — Reader Profile

Date: 2026-05-10

## Level and Language

- **Level**: A1 (beginner)
- **Vocabulary control**: 80% top-1500 frequency words + 20% new words (surfaced in keywords list)
- **Dialect**: neutral Latin American (no *vosotros*, no strong regionalisms)

## What Hooks the Reader

### Genres (priority order)
1. **Hard sci-fi** — realistic physics, engineering solutions (Expanse, Andy Weir)
2. **Magical realism** — ordinary world + the strange (Borges, Márquez)
3. **Cyberpunk / dystopia** — dark futures
4. **Popular science**: AI/programming, space/astronomy
5. **History**: ancient civilizations, Middle Ages/Renaissance, 20th–21st century, Spanish-speaking world

### Core narrative hooks
- **Observer in an alien world** (cf. Strugatsky's *Hard to Be a God*)
- **Moral dilemmas** with no clean right answer
- **Ideas about progress and societal development**
- **Outsider observing humans** (cf. Matt Haig's *The Humans* — alien-narrator)

### Tone
- **Even 50/50 mix**: serious/dark alternates with ironic/absurd
- Atmosphere palette: dark/noir, cozy, ironic, epic — all welcome

## Anti-vibe (kills interest fast)

- Heavy-handed moralizing
- Romance subplots
- Childish naivety (typical "learner" simplification)
- Excessive mundane realism (implicit — fits the same pattern)

## Shared Universe: Space Expansion

All texts live in one universe (lore accumulates), but each text is **self-contained, no cliffhangers**.

Sub-vibes (mixed across stories):
- Hard sci-fi realism (physics, engineering)
- Exploration frontier (first contact, expeditions, mysteries)
- Colonies and societies (politics, daily life, conflicts)
- Intimate crew stories (psychology in confined spaces)

**Scientific plausibility**: plausible with allowances. Cryosleep, FTL, terraforming OK if internally consistent.

### Protagonists (rotation)
- Engineer / technician / programmer
- Scientist / researcher
- Navigator / captain / pilot
- AI / non-human intelligence

POV: 1st or 3rd person — varies per text.

## Format

- **Length**: 150-300 words (~3-5 min reading)
- **Structure**: complete mini-story, no cliffhangers
- **Pace**: mixed (depends on genre)
- **Ending**: any — open, twist, closed
- **Cultural setting**: any (Spanish-world setting is a bonus, not a requirement)

## Delivery

- **Platform**: browser on PC (local HTML)
- **Text**: Spanish only
- **UI**: hover on a word → popup with translation, basic grammar info (part of speech, form), and a link to spanishdict.com
- **After text**: 5-10 keywords for memorization (focus list)
- **Avoid**: grammar breakdowns, comprehension questions, full text translations

## Cadence

- **On demand** — user requests next text, generator delivers
- Not a subscription, not a calendar
